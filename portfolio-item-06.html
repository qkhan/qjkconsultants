Federal Court of Australia - Fuzzy Matching / Data Enhancement

(AustralAsian Legal Information Institute - AUSTLII)

The Australasian Legal Information Institute (AustLII) provides free internet access to Australasian legal materials. AustLII's broad public policy agenda is to improve access to justice through better access to information. So it has become one of the largest sources of legal materials on the net, with over four million searchable documents. AustLII is Australia's most popular online free-access resource for Australian legal information, serving the needs of a multitude of users with over 700,000 hits daily.


Project Overview


  1. Many of the old legal records of the institute from the year 1977 to 1996 used embedded citations to quote references but the problem was that, most of them didnâ€™t have any references at all. Such was the condition of about 12 years of legal data and the remaining data of 8 years had incomplete citations.

  2. The institute wanted to streamline the data and enhance its quality by assigning complete and accurate cogitations for 20 years of data.

  3. The initial work performed to assign the embedded citation, only worked for a few of years. So, the goal was to develop a software system that could find out and map correct citations for text in a document.

  4. The system must be able to complete the incomplete references and link complete references for the documents without references to the correct locations.

  5. The system had to be designed for the compete 20 years of data.

Developed Solution


  1. All the data under scope was in the form of HTML files. In order to develop the software a database dump file that contained all the correct citations for all the HTML documents (provided by the institute) was used as reference to match against the embedded citations with in the HTML files. But this reference was also not totally accurate as it was compiled by the data entry department of the institute and there was a considerable probability of errors in it.

  2. A complex citation matching algorithm was developed to match and correct the citations in the HTML files with reference database dump. The algorithm used a variety of key citation details extracted from the original HTML documents and uses them to find correct citation matches. In order to verify the correctness of already existing citations, a number of contextual pattern matching checks were to used.

  3. This data was than matched with the imperfect database data using various fuzzy matching algorithms, identifying best matches and selecting the right match, based on highest Match Frequency. The updated information is added to the Registry file, allowing reader to access correct Citation for a particular case.

  4. The software implementing the algorithm was developed using Advanced Object Oriented Perl, Linux and Shell Scripting and completely eliminates wrong or missing citations in the data both efficiently and also in much less time as compared to the manual approach.
