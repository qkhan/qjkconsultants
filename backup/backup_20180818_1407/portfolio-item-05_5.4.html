Process Overview


    Design & developed new “Payment Management and Reporting” system for the finance department. The new system has reduced the reliance of the finance department over the claims team, for accounts and payment related issues, thus allowing the two teams to function simultaneously, without waiting for the response from each other.

  1. The project is initiated to de-couple general ledger software from the insurance claims system, enabling new payment management system to handle accounting related enquiries, generate ABA files, enable finance / underwriting personnel to view/download various analytical reports from the web portal.


  2. The new system has allowed visibility and audit trail of the payment related tasks. Finance officer can select number of payment extractions and generate the bank (ABA) file. The finance team is able to view and download various analytical reports from Fipro. Individual payments are not be tracked via MYOB. The new system has reduced the duplication of the data. Only policy totals will be added to the General Ledger Software, MYOB (for auditing and other tax related issues).

Process Overview


  1. The claims officer process a benefit payment for the claimant using the screen below

  2. The claims officer selects the payment start and the end date and press “Calculate”

  3. The payment is calculated, deriving number of days to be paid, the Net amount, tax amount (based on the tax calculator from ATO), the super amount (if super is applicaple), the Gross amount, which is Net + Tax and the total Benefit Liability, which is the sum of Net, Tax and Super.

  4. When the claim officer processes the payment transaction (via InterClaim), it is added to the payment confirmation queue.

  5. The claims supervisor authorizes the transaction and adds to the active payment extraction queue.


  6. The payment extraction process is run; the payment summary detail is generated via InterClaim and is verified by the supervisor.

  7. The extraction occurs and batch of payments selected are extracted




  8. The extraction 1993 is now available to the finance via FIPRO “Payment Management Interface” screen


  9. The finance officer can click on the hyperlink “1993” and find number of payments grouped under the extraction ID.


  10. If the user wants to check an individual payment Id, it can click on the payment ID, for instance: 68347 and the system shows the payment related details.


  11. The user goes back to the “Payment Management Interface” screen and extracts the report available to do reconciliation, the two reports “Policy Journal” and “Organisation Journal” are used for the reconciliation of payments with General Ledger.


  12. Finance officer than clicks on the “ABA file” and find separate ABA (bank) files per binder facility.


  13. The ABA file is than uploaded to the Internet bank web site and payment made to the clients


  14. The finance officer then goes to the extraction payment list and mark them paid.


  15. The finance officer than goes to the payment list for the extraction and marks them “Paid”


  16. The finance officer can bounce a payment, after marking them paid, if the payment sent via ABA file is bounced



  17. The payment cancelled is informed to the claims team via task management system.


  18. The extraction id 1993 is shown as “Marked Paid” on the payment management interface for record management.


  19. Similar interface is designed for the expense payments and cancelled payments.


Key System Functionality

  1. The system provides all kinds of insurance claims
  2. The system provides the flexible connectivity essential to effective claims management.
  3. It reduces time spent doing routine administrative tasks.
